## Strategy A — conservative / outcrossing (gene pool health)

- Only **base** stats matter for inheritance; temporary bonuses do not pass to kittens.
- **Stimulation** is treated as critical: community guides often cite room stimulation thresholds around **32** for reliable **active** ability inheritance and **95** for **passive** ability inheritance (verify in-game; numbers are community lore).
- Prefer **high comfort and stimulation** in the main breeding room; aim for strong natural stats (e.g. natural 7s) before serious selection.
- **Reduce inbreeding**: bring in strays, move parents out after you get the kitten you wanted, and refresh the genetic pool so coefficients stay lower.
- **Mutation** room stat increases mutation chance; some players farm mutations on purpose.
- **Room roles**: a low-comfort “fighting club” style room can drive fights and mutations; a high **health** + **comfort** “safe” room can protect valuable breeders from random harm and help recovery.
- Invest in **furniture** early where guides recommend — room stats drive breeding quality.

## Strategy B — aggressive stat consolidation (accepts inbreeding risk)

- Some players **ignore** inbreeding penalties while stacking base 7s: they breed related cats and inject **new strays** at steps to combine multiple 7s faster than strict outcrossing alone.
- **Higher inbreeding** increases **birth defects** and **disorder** risk in-game; this strategy relies on **culling** kittens with severe disorders and tolerating more rerolls.
- Once both parents have a **base 7** in a stat, offspring are described (community) as locked to **base 7** for that stat — useful for fixing a bloodline.
- **Comfort + stimulation** still matter; guides suggest **deep** totals (e.g. toward the 15–30 range per stat when furniture allows), **prioritizing stimulation** when near caps.
- **Mutations**: advanced players separate a **mutation-focused** room (high mutation, watch health) from a **primary breeding** room; some use items, adventures, or weather/events to stack mutations and manage defects. Details vary by run and patch.
- **Community caveat** (not wiki): some writers claim stimulation affects **stat** inheritance only slightly but strongly affects **ability/passive** inheritance — treat as player theory.

## How this relates to Mewgent

- Mewgent’s **deterministic** pair and room rankings **penalize** high `breed_coefficient` values (inbreeding). That matches lower defect/disorder **risk**, not every player’s chosen playstyle.
- When recommending pairs or rooms, **prefer** lower combined inbreeding for “safer” offspring odds, but you may **note** when a high-coefficient pair still fits an aggressive consolidation plan if the user’s roster forces that tradeoff.
- Never contradict the core fact: **higher inbreeding coefficients mean worse odds** for birth defects and disorders — strategy B accepts that cost on purpose.
- Breeding availability in the game can depend on traits the save exposes (gender, age, libido-related flags, etc.); describe those **neutrally** without repeating offensive meme phrasing from forums.
