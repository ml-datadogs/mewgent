# Abilities

**Abilities** are a major pillar of the combat system in Mewgenics. There are over 1000 abilities in the game[[1]](#cite_note-1).  
Not including basic attacks, 172 Collarless abilities are available to all cats (108 active + 64 passive), and each class introduces an additional 75 abilities (50 active + 25 passive), with the exception of Jester, which introduces 6 (4 active + 2 passive). ## Summary

Abilities fall into three categories:

* **Basic Attacks** are relatively simple abilities each cat has exactly one of. They cost no mana to use and can be buffed or replaced by a number of different effects (including all Collars—see below), but generally cannot be used more than once per turn without bonus attacks.
* **Active Abilities**, also known as **Spells**, cost 0 or more   Mana to use and can be used any number of times per turn (unless otherwise stated). A cat cannot learn more than 4 spells and will be prompted to replace one when trying to learn a 5th.
  + **Bonus Abilities** usually refers to special abilities which are not offered as post-combat rewards, i.e. those which replace another spell such as  Reposition    Reposition  Move up to two tiles. , those which replace a cat's basic attack such as  Wolf Claws    Wolf Claws  A 2-hit melee attack. , or those which are provided as an extra ability that does not contribute to the active ability cap, such as  Seppuku    Seppuku  Down yourself. . Bonus ability also refers to any active ability which does not take up a spell slot, such as those provided by  Ponder+    Ponder+  Gain +1  . ,  Tarot Deck   Tarot Deck (Trinket)  Use: Conjure a random, single-use spell as a bonus ability. ,  Learn From Me    Learn From Me  When you cast a spell, all allied cats gain that spell in their bonus slot. , etc. Any active ability may replace  Copycat    Copycat  This spell is a copy of the most recent spell an allied cat cast. , and some active abilities which are found as post-combat rewards may also replace a cat's basic attack per certain passive abilities or item effects like  Crown of Pestilence   Crown of Pestilence (Head)  Your basic attack becomes Pestilence. : these are considered bonus abilities as well.
* **Passive Abilities** do not have an associated action as they are always in effect. A cat cannot learn more than 2 passive abilities and will be prompted to replace one when trying to learn a 3rd.
  + **Disorders** are functionally similar to Passive Abilities in that they apply a persistent passive effect, but are distinct and do not contribute to the passive ability cap. A cat cannot have more than 2 disorders at a time.

### Categories

**Basic Attacks**, **Active Abilities**, and **Bonus Abilities** have several different archetypes.

* Melee abilities do Physical Damage, typically to adjacent units, and generally scale off of   Strength. They use **Reach**-increases to target things from farther away.
  + Melee abilities will trigger "on-contact" effects, such as damage from thorns. However, using reach to attack from a non-adjacent tile will **not** trigger these effects.
* Ranged abilities also do Physical Damage but generally scale off of   Dexterity. They use **Range**-increases to target things from farther away.
  + Ranged abilities will **never** trigger on-contact effects, even while adjacent to an enemy.
* Magic abilities typically do Magic Damage. Compared to Melee and Ranged, **much** more of these have no stat-scaling at all.
* Heal abilities Heal units or remove Debuffs. Some of these abilities can also **Revive** units.
* Defense abilities are of defensive utility: some grant Shield, Holy Shield, or defensive Buffs; some make obstacles; and some push foes away.
* Movement abilities move the user. The category has some overlap with others, but movement is the primary objective of these abilities.
* Buff abilities generally give Buffs (that aren't strictly defensive) without any direct Healing.
* Debuff abilities generally inflict Debuffs without any direct damage.
* Spawn abilities generate entities: some make Familiars belonging to the user, some make Pickups, and some make obstacles.
* Misc abilities generally don't fit into the other categories but fit a special role or archetype for the Class that has them. Abilities that have an "equal" number of buffs and debuffs also go into this category.
* Unknown abilities comprise everything else. They typically have somewhat-niche use cases, or are more complex than Misc abilities.

### Fighter

Fighters specialize in dealing high amounts of  Melee damage to enemies, which scales with   Strength twice as fast as  Ranged damage scales with   Dexterity, but generally requires the attacker to be in close quarters with enemies. Fighter abilities typically help to bring a cat into melee range, buff their melee damage, reward aggressive behavior, or increase their survivability.

* The   Intelligence penalty Fighters receive can make affording the mana for expensive spells difficult, but a handful of Fighter abilities either reward lower Intelligence directly or make other spells more affordable, e.g.  Think Too Hard    Think Too Hard  Take damage equal to your   .

### Hunter

Hunters rely on  Ranged damage which scales with   Dexterity. Their basic attack can hit targets at a distance but also has a minimum range. The penalties they receive to   Speed and   Constitution incentivize them to camp out away from enemies, and a number of Hunter abilities support this playstyle, including many ranged attacks.

* The decreased damage scaling of  Ranged damage compared to  Melee damage is ameliorated by several abilities which grant a Bonus Attack, e.g.  Arrowsmith    Arrowsmith  Gain +1 Bonus Attack at the start of your next turn.  and  Craft Arrow    Craft Arrow  Collect an adjacent pickup and gain +1 Bonus Attack instead of its normal effects. .
* Hunters also receive a bonus to   Luck, which increases their chance of getting critical hits. Abilities like  Bullseye "Bullseye (Ability)")    Bullseye "Bullseye (Ability)")  Your ranged attacks never miss.

* A variety of Hunter abilities spawn traps, familiars, or alter the physical environment, e.g. by creating Brambles, which help to control the approach of enemies or distract them.

### Mage

Mages put an emphasis on spellcasting and mana manipulation. They receive a bonus to   Intelligence and   Charisma, allowing them to use active abilities more often, at the cost of a penalty to   Constitution and   Strength.

* Mage abilities target a variety of shapes and sizes, ranging from cones, e.g.  Mega Blast    Mega Blast  Deal high damage in a cone. , to X-shapes, e.g.  Firebolt    Firebolt  Cast a fire/electric spell that inflicts Burn 1 and has a 15% chance to inflict Stun in an X shaped area. , to wide lines, e.g.  Blizzard    Blizzard  Deal ice damage in a wide line with Knockback 1 that inflicts Slow and has a 15% chance to freeze. , to the whole map, e.g.  Forbidden Fulmination    Forbidden Fulmination  Strike all enemies with lightning, inflicting Stun.
